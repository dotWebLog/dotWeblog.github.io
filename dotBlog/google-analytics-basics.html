> Google Analytics Basics

Google Analytics can seem overwhelming at first, but understanding the basics sets you on the right track to mastering this powerful tool. It's a free web service that tracks and analyses data about your website visitors. It tells you who your visitors are, where they come from, what they do on your site, and how they convert into leads or customers. It is just a powerful tool that provides valuable insights into the performance of your website or app.

Letâ€™s discover the basics to help you understand and navigate Google Analytics:

  1. Setting Up Google Analytics:
    • Create an Account:
      • Go to Google Analytics and sign in with your Google account.
      • Click on "Start for free" to create an account.
    • Create a Property:
      • Add a new property for your website or app within your Google Analytics account.
    • Get Tracking Code:
      • After creating a property, you'll receive a tracking code.
      • Copy and paste this code into the header of your website or app.
  2. Understanding Google Analytics Dashboard:
    • Home:
      • The default landing page provides a snapshot of your website's key metrics.
    • Realtime:
      • Shows current user activity on your site.
    • Audience:
      • Provides insights into your users, including demographics, location, and devices.
    • Acquisition:
      • Shows how users find and access your website, whether through search engines, social media, or other sources.
    • Behavior:
      • Analyses user engagement and behavior on your site, including popular pages and how users navigate.
    • Conversions:
      • Tracks specific goals or actions completed by users, such as form submissions or product purchases.
  3. Key Metrics and Dimensions:
    • Metrics:
      • Quantifiable measurements like sessions, pageviews, bounce rate, and conversion rate.
    • Dimensions:
      • Descriptive attributes like source/medium, device, location, and page.
  4. Common Metrics:
    • Sessions:
      • The total number of visits to your site.
    • Pageviews:
      • The total number of pages viewed by users.
    • Bounce Rate:
      • The percentage of single-page sessions.
    • Conversion Rate:
      • The percentage of users who completed a desired action.
  5. Reports and Analysis:
    • Custom Reports:
      • Create personalised reports based on specific metrics and dimensions.
    • Segments:
      • Analyse specific subsets of your data to gain deeper insights.
  6. Goals and Conversions:
    • Set Up Goals:
      • Define and track specific actions as goals (e.g., form submissions, product purchases).
    • E-commerce Tracking:
      • If applicable, set up e-commerce tracking for online stores.
  7. Annotations:
    • Add annotations to mark significant events or changes on your timeline for better context during analysis.
  8. Custom Alerts:
    • Set up alerts to be notified of unusual or significant changes in your site's performance.
  9. Learning Resources:
    • Google Analytics Academy:
      • Offers free courses to deepen your understanding.
    • Community and Documentation:
      • Explore the Google Analytics Help Center and community forums for additional support.
  10. Regular Review and Optimisation:
    • Regularly review your reports to identify trends and areas for improvement.
    • Use the insights to optimise your website or app for better user experience and performance.

Google Analytics is a crucial tool for understanding the performance of your website or app. By exploring the steps outlined above, you can set up your account, navigate the dashboard, and utilise key metrics to gain valuable insights into user behavior. Customisation options, such as setting up goals, e-commerce tracking, and creating custom reports, allow you to tailor your analysis to specific business objectives.
