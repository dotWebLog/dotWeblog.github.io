> Low-Tech Success

March 2025

In an era where technology dominates nearly every industry, some companies have chosen a different path—one rooted in simplicity, craftsmanship, and tradition. While businesses worldwide invest in artificial intelligence, automation, and digital tools, a select few have proven that success is still possible without the latest gadgets. These companies have thrived by relying on minimal technology, preserving old-world techniques, and maintaining a personal touch. Below, we explore a few inspiring case studies of businesses that have flourished without cutting-edge technology.

In a digital world where smartphones, tablets, and apps dominate, one company has built an empire on an age-old practice: writing by hand. Leuchtturm1917, a German notebook manufacturer, has thrived by producing high-quality, traditional journals for those who appreciate the tactile experience of putting pen to paper.

Founded in 1917, the company has remained committed to crafting premium notebooks that cater to professionals, writers, and artists. Despite the rise of note-taking apps and digital planning tools, Leuchtturm1917 has grown significantly in the past decade, proving that people still crave the simplicity and focus that comes with handwriting. By prioritizing quality materials, thoughtful design, and durability, the company has built a loyal customer base worldwide.

Brooks England, a British company founded in 1866, has remained a powerhouse in the cycling industry by crafting leather bicycle saddles using time-honored techniques. While modern bicycle manufacturers focus on synthetic materials, lightweight carbon fiber, and futuristic designs, Brooks has continued to rely on traditional methods that date back over a century.

Their leather saddles, which mold to the rider’s shape over time, offer unparalleled comfort and durability. Brooks has embraced a philosophy of sustainability and craftsmanship, appealing to cyclists who appreciate timeless design over technological advancements. The brand’s commitment to quality and heritage has allowed it to maintain relevance despite ever-changing industry trends.

Located in Kentucky, the Shaker Village of Pleasant Hill is a remarkable example of a business thriving by preserving the past. The historic site operates as a working community, producing handcrafted furniture, woven textiles, and artisanal goods without modern machinery. The Shaker craftsmanship, known for its simplicity and durability, continues to attract customers who seek authenticity in a mass-produced world.

The village also serves as an educational center, demonstrating self-sufficient living, sustainable agriculture, and handcrafting techniques that date back centuries. By focusing on heritage tourism, high-quality handmade goods, and sustainable practices, the Shaker Village has become a model for businesses that wish to succeed without heavy reliance on modern technology.

In China, the century-old brand Ronghua Xiang has become a cultural icon by producing handcrafted folding fans using traditional techniques. While mass production and automation have taken over many industries, this company has remained committed to handcrafting each fan with intricate designs and delicate craftsmanship.

Despite digital printing and factory-made alternatives, Ronghua Xiang’s dedication to quality has attracted collectors and enthusiasts from around the world. The company’s success stems from its emphasis on tradition, artistry, and cultural preservation, proving that handmade goods still have a place in modern markets.

These case studies illustrate that success does not always require the latest technology. In a world increasingly driven by automation and digitalization, companies like Leuchtturm1917, Brooks England, Shaker Village, and Ronghua Xiang have shown that traditional methods, craftsmanship, and personal connection can still lead to thriving businesses. By prioritizing quality, authenticity, and timeless appeal, these businesses have carved out a niche that technology simply cannot replicate.
