> Review: Substack and Medium

Choosing between Substack and Medium depends on your specific needs, goals, and preferences. Both platforms have distinct features and cater to different types of writers.

Let’s start with Substack:

  1. Newsletter Focus:
    • Substack is primarily designed for newsletters. It allows you to build and grow your audience through email subscriptions.
    • If your main goal is to connect directly with readers through email and build a subscriber base, Substack might be a better fit.
  2. Monetisation:
    • Substack offers easy monetisation options, allowing writers to charge for subscriptions. This can be appealing if you're looking to generate income from your content.
    • It provides a straightforward way to offer premium content to paying subscribers.
  3. Simplicity:
    • Substack is known for its simplicity. It's easy to set up, and the focus is on creating and delivering content without getting bogged down by complex features.

Now, let’s look at Medium:

  1. Diverse Content:
    • Medium is a more diverse platform that supports various types of content, including articles, stories, and blog posts.
    • If you want to publish a mix of short-form and long-form content and reach a broader audience, Medium may be a better choice.
  2. Built-In Audience:
    • Medium has a built-in audience and a social aspect that can help your content reach a wider readership.
    • If exposure and discoverability are important to you, Medium's community might be beneficial.
  3. Partner Program:
    • Medium offers a Partner Program where writers can earn money based on engagement from Medium subscribers. This is an additional monetisation option.
    • If you want to explore different ways of earning income, the Medium Partner Program can be an attractive feature.

Here are some considerations when it comes to choosing which is best:

  1. Control and Ownership:
    • With Substack, you have more control over your email list and direct communication with subscribers.
    • Medium owns the platform, and changes in policies can affect how your content is distributed. Consider whether you're comfortable with the level of control each platform provides.
  2. Cost:
    • Substack and Medium both have free options, but Substack charges a percentage for paid subscriptions. Medium's Partner Program involves a subscription fee, but the core platform is free.
  3. Goals and Audience:
    • Define your goals and target audience. If you're focused on building a dedicated subscriber base, Substack might be preferable. If you want broader visibility and engagement, Medium might be a better fit.

To wrap up:

The choice between Substack and Medium depends on your specific blogging and content goals. Here's a brief summary to help you make an informed decision:

  • Choose Substack if:
    • Your main focus is on building a direct relationship with your audience through newsletters.
    • Monetisation through paid subscriptions is a priority.
    • You prefer a simple platform with a straightforward setup.
  • Choose Medium if:
    • You want to publish a variety of content types, including articles, stories, and blog posts.
    • Exposure to a broader audience and leveraging the Medium community is important to you.
    • Exploring different monetisation options, such as the Medium Partner Program, is appealing.
  • Considerations for Both:
    • Evaluate your level of control and ownership over your content and audience.
    • Factor in the cost structure, including any fees associated with monetisation on each platform.
    • Clearly define your blogging goals and target audience to align with the strengths of the chosen platform.

Ultimately, the best platform for you depends on your unique preferences, content strategy, and desired outcomes. It might also be worth experimenting with both platforms to see which one aligns better with your blogging style and goals over time.
